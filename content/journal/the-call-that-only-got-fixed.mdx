There's a pattern that keeps repeating in the WNBA: a hard foul on Caitlin Clark goes uncalled in real time, the clip spreads online, and only then — hours later, under pressure — does the league revisit the play. Sometimes the call is upgraded after the fact. Sometimes it isn't. Either way, the decision that mattered wasn't made when it counted, on the floor, by the people paid to make it.

That's not officiating working. That's officiating failing in real time and getting relitigated after the fact — after the backlash, after the clips, after the pressure. WNBA referees, notably, aren't even full-time employees of the league.

This is the exact failure mode *Unwhistled* was written to expose: a broken officiating infrastructure that only self-corrects once the internet forces its hand. The call was missed by the people paid to see it. It was only fixed by people who saw it on a phone screen after the fact.

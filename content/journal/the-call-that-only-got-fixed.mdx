On June 24th, a foul happened that no one called live. It took slow-motion replay, hours later, for the league to see what actually happened on the court between Caitlin Clark and Alyssa Thomas. Only after the game did freeze-frame review show contact to Clark's throat, and only then did the league retroactively upgrade the play to a Flagrant Foul 2 and suspend Thomas for the next game.

That's not officiating working. That's officiating failing in real time and getting corrected after the fact — after the backlash, after the clips, after the pressure. WNBA referees, notably, aren't even full-time employees of the league.

This is the exact failure mode *Unwhistled* was written to expose: a broken officiating infrastructure that only self-corrects once the internet forces its hand. The call was missed by the people paid to see it. It was only fixed by people who saw it on a phone screen after the fact.
